  ### For a SIM Card:
  1. **Local ID Requirement**: Usually, you need a Turkish identity card or a foreigner’s residence permit. However, some providers might accept your passport in certain instances.
  2. **Documentation**: You typically need to present your identification and possibly provide proof of your address.
  3. **Purchase Process**: You can visit a mobile phone provider's store, show your documents, and buy a prepaid SIM card. Some providers may offer additional options for foreigners.

  ### For a Bank Account:
  1. **Identification**: Most banks will require a Turkish ID or a residence permit. Some banks may allow you to open a basic account with just a passport, but this varies by bank.
  2. **Additional Documentation**: You may need proof of address, such as a utility bill or rental agreement, and potentially a tax identification number from the Turkish tax office.
  3. **Visiting a Bank**: To open an account, you typically have to go to a branch and present the required documents. It's advisable to check with the bank in advance about their specific requirements for foreigners.

  ### Without a Turkish ID:
  If you do not possess a Turkish ID or residence permit:
  - **SIM Card**: You might have difficulty obtaining a SIM card, as most providers are stringent about identification requirements. Checking for providers that specifically cater to tourists or allow passport usage might help.
