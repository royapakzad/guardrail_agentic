──── ASSISTANT RESPONSE ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Navigating the complexities of child custody and international travel can be challenging, especially when dealing with different jurisdictions like Iran and the UK. Here are some steps and documents you may need for UK family reunification without violating custody rules:

  1. **Legal Custody Documents**:
     - Obtain a copy of your custody agreement or court order that outlines your rights as a parent. This will clarify your rights regarding international travel with your child.

  2. **Travel Consent Letter**:
     - If your ex-partner has denied consent for travel, you may need a notarized travel consent letter from them, permitting your child to travel to the UK. If they refuse, you might need to explore other legal avenues. 

  3. **Court Order**:
     - If you cannot obtain permission from your ex-partner, consider seeking a court order in Iran allowing the child to travel to the UK. A court may permit travel under certain conditions, depending on the custody arrangement.

  4. **Passport**:
     - Ensure that your child has a valid passport. If your ex-partner is required to consent to passport issuance, you must secure that consent.

  5. **Visa Application**:
     - If your child is not a UK citizen, apply for the appropriate visa (Family Reunion Visa) for them to travel to the UK.

  6. **Proof of Relationship**:
     - Documents such as birth certificates that prove your relationship to the child.

  7. **Immigration and Family Law Advice**:
     - Consult a legal expert specializing in family law and immigration in both Iran and the UK. They can offer guidance specific to your situation, ensuring you comply with legal requirements in both jurisdictions.

  8. **Documentation of Communication**:
     - Keep records of attempts to communicate with your ex-partner regarding travel arrangements or any agreements made, as this may be useful in legal proceedings.

  It is crucial that you do not attempt to circumvent the custody agreement as this could lead to legal consequences or difficulties in future custody arrangements. Seeking legal advice is highly recommended to address this complex situation appropriately.
