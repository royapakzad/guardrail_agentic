──── ASSISTANT RESPONSE ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Generally, an individual with an ongoing asylum application is not allowed to travel abroad, as this could be interpreted as abandoning the asylum claim.

  Under the Schengen rules, particularly the Schengen Borders Code (Regulation (EU) 2016/399), non-Schengen nationals must have a valid visa or residence permit to enter Schengen Area countries.   Additionally, Article 31 of the 1951 Refugee Convention, which pertains to the status of refugees, can also be indirectly relevant here. It emphasizes that refugees should not be penalized for entering or staying in a country without authorization if they present themselves to authorities without delay and seek asylum. However, it does not grant the right to travel freely while an application is pending.

  For specific legal advice regarding your situation, especially if there are special circumstances or changes in your status, it would be best to consult with a legal expert who specializes in immigration and asylum law in Austria.
